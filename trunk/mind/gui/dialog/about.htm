The development of the software reMIND has been going on since 2000. The method that reMIND is based upon is the MIND method, which was developed in the early 1990’s at the division Energy Systems at Linköping university. Originally the MIND method was implemented in a Fortran-based software, simply called MIND. Due to several reasons the Fortran-based software needed to be replaced, which was enabled by the offspring reMIND.

The development has been going on in several rounds with the supervision of the division Energy Systems at Linköping university. At Energy Systems a group of people has been involved in e.g. creating requirements for functions, for the graphical interface and for the overall functioning of the software. This group consisted at the initial step of the following persons:

Ph.D Magnus Karlsson
Ph.D Peter Sandberg

The initial programming was performed by a student group at Linköping university building the graphical interface and implemented the basic functions for the software. This group started creating reMIND software in the beginning of 2001 and delivered the first version in May 2001. The student group consisted of:

Johan Trygg and Tim Terlegård continued working on the software during the following summer including some additional functions to the software and corrected some errors.

After the initial work at Linköping university a group at Luleå technical university included two new functions (Flow Equation and BoundaryTOP) and corrected some errors. In this group Ph.D Mikael Larsson formulated the requirements and Urban Liljedahl implemented the ideas in the software.

In late summer 2003 the software was updated once more, where a variety of changes were performed such as; errors were corrected, the graphical interface was improved, the structure of the software program was enhanced and the function of time steps was developed. At this stage Magnus Karlsson and Peter Sandberg at Linköping university constituted the group that formulated the requirements, while a consulting firm, ACE Simulation, implemented the requests. At ACE Simulation Jonas Sääv and Hans-Fredric Österlund did the programming. Peter Andersson, from the student group, was used as support at the initial stage of this work, likewise was Mats Söderström.

A new student group was engaged to develop reMIND further during the autumn 2003 under the supervision of Magnus Karlsson and Peter Sandberg at Linköping university. In parallel, at Luleå technical university, the development of reMIND continued by including new functions in the software. This mainly included the opportunity for a multi-objective approach and the function editor. Urban Liljedahl, Jan Sköllermark, Nils-Oskar Spett, Rickard Harju and Marcus Bergendorff implemented this in a local version at first, under the supervision of mainly Mikael Larsson at Luleå technical university with the assistant of Peter Sandberg at Linköping university. In spring 2005 this was merged into reMIND by Marcus Bergendorff.

The next update was implemented by the consulting firm ACE Simulation, Jonas Sääv, in spring 2005. Based on the earlier implemented automatic routine, lp_solve was included as a possible optimizer as a complement to CPLEX, which was the only optimizer available before. Also, the time for creation of the MPS-file was reduced by changes within the code and some corrections of errors where made. During these changes Magnus Karlsson acted as supervisor.

Ph.D student Nawzad Mardan was engaged to develop reMIND further during the summer 2007
